Buddhism
Documentary

Buddhism is a spiritual tradition and philosophy that originated in ancient India around the 5th century BCE with Siddhartha Gautama, known as the Buddha. It teaches the path to enlightenment (Nirvana) through practices and principles such as meditation, ethical conduct, and wisdom. Central to Buddhism are the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, which guide followers in understanding the nature of suffering and how to overcome it.

The Chi of Tea
Documentary

The Chi of Tea: "A Tranquil Journey" delves into the ancient art and serene ritual of tea drinking, a practice steeped in history and culture, spanning thousands of years. Originating in China, the tradition of tea has traversed continents, embodying not just a beverage but a form of meditation and connection. The documentary explores how the simple act of preparing and savoring tea can be a gateway to mindfulness, tranquility, and harmony with nature.

The Latter Years
Documentary

Ageing: The Inevitable Journey" explores the universal and inevitable process of ageing, a journey that every living being embarks upon from the moment of birth. This natural progression brings about changes in physical, mental, and social aspects of life. As we age, our bodies gradually slow down, marked by wrinkles and gray hair, while our wisdom and experience grow.

Potters' View
Documentary

"Potters' View" offers a glimpse into the world of pottery, a craft steeped in tradition and creativity. This short documentary captures the essence of pottery-making, showcasing the skill and passion of potters as they shape clay into beautiful, functional pieces of art.

Protect the Future
Documentary

Protect the Future: A Pledge for Children" is an inspiring, succinct documentary that shines a light on the importance of creating a sustainable, healthy world for our children. It emphasizes the idea that children are the future, inheriting the planet and its challenges.
